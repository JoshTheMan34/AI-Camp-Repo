How it works
The Team

Our model Andrew uses filtered data of every Yu-Gi-Oh card to generate a completely random card which can be used in a real Yu-Gi-Oh match!

To get our data, we filtered out all cards with the “Pendulum Effect” which complicated the card battle, filtered out all cards which weren't monsters, organized the cards based on their attribute, and formatted the output to be:

﹤name﹥~desc~*ATK; DEF; LEVEL; RACE.

We are using aitextgen to train our model, using "EleutherAI/gpt-neo-125M". Training parameters of our model:

Training Epochs: 3000; Learning rate: 0.001; Batch size: 2

The training time of each model is around 50 minutes, and the total training time was about 5 hrs for all models (dark, light, fire, water, earth, wind).

We also tried training our model with 1000 and 5000 training steps. The issue with a 1000 step model is that the result isn't random. While the 5000 step model is so random that the generated results are incoherent.

To generate the custom Yu-gi-oh card, we need to collect image assets. We used PIL to crop the image datasets from all Yu-gi-oh cards, categorizing them by attribute and race. Then the image assets will combine with our generated texts and properties, and sent to https://www.cardmaker.net/yugioh/ to produce custom Yu-gi-oh card.

Due to the storage limitation, each combination of attribute and race, we only collected 5 pictures. The system will randomly pick one out of these 5 pictures for generation.

For our website, we started with an online template and built off of it using html. As we kept on working on our project, we also implemented some CSS and JS code to clean up the website as we built the card generator and card battle completely from scratch. Examples of our work on the website can even be seen in the features like card gen,card battle, and even the side scrolling bar your using right now.

We used multiple different references and strategies in order to create the card gen/battle that you can now use. Even the way that the battle would have played out and the rules that would've been used was tested and altered until we got our final product.

Challenges:

The first challenge we ran into was finding a large set of Yu-gi-oh card data that could be read by the AI. After searching, we found an excel spread sheet that gave us all the information we needed.

The second challenge we faced was that we didn't know what kind of AI model would work the best for the kind of project we were striving to make.

The third challenge we had was creating the card battles. The logic of the card battles was extremely tricky and took a lot of time and dedication to properly fix.
